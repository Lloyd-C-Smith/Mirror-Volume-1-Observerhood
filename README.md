# Mirror Volume I — Observerhood

This repository contains **Volume I — Observerhood from Constraint, Viability and Recursive Reliability**, the first substantive research volume of the Mirror Programme.

Volume I investigates the conditions under which a bounded physical or computational system becomes an observer-like locus of modelled reality. Across theory papers, mathematical formalisation, computational laboratories and physics-support papers, the volume develops observerhood as a derived organisational condition grounded in constraint, viability, recursive reliability, memory governance and physically supported continuity under perturbation.

## Central Thesis

Observerhood is not primitive. It is a derived organisational condition in which a bounded system preserves viability by maintaining world-models, self-models, reliability estimates, memory governance and physically supported continuity under perturbation.

## Volume Architecture

Volume I is organised into four completed arcs.

### 1. Theory Arc

The Theory Arc develops the conceptual and philosophical foundation of recursive observerhood. It introduces the programme's core refusal to treat observerhood, selfhood or representation as primitive explanatory atoms where they can instead be derived from distinguishability, bounded modelling, viability, self-modelling and recursive reliability.

### 2. Mathematical Formalisation Arc

The Mathematical Formalisation Arc develops the constraint calculus, minimal observer thresholds and identity-continuity formalism. It makes explicit the conditions under which self-model reliability becomes action-guiding, cost-sensitive and viability-relevant.

### 3. Computational Observerhood Labs

The Computational Observerhood Labs provide controlled simulations of reliability, repair, recursive reliability, estimator corruption, neuro-symbolic persistence and autobiographical continuity. The labs are not claims of consciousness. They are reproducible toy environments testing whether the formal structures improve viability under specified perturbation regimes.

### 4. Physics Support Arc

The Physics Support Arc identifies the physical support conditions required for persistent observerhood: causal order, finite propagation, local substrate persistence, metastable memory, thermodynamic throughput, error correction, constraint salience, maintenance cost, causal invariance and temporal asymmetry. These papers do not modify relativity, quantum theory or thermodynamics. They state conservative compatibility and support conditions for physically realised observer-continuity.
